I am a Research Assistant at India Autism Center and at Institute of Neuroscience Kolkata, where I am working with Prof. Bhismadev Chakrabarti and Dr. Hrishikesh Kumar on understanding the phenotypic overlap between Parkinson's Disease (PD) and Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD).

I am broadly interested in stuyding and research at the intersection of Cognitive Science (Vision and Language Acquisition) and Psychology. My current research interests particularly lie in understand the role of color in vision and consciousness (dreams, etc.).

Prior to this, I graduated with as a Young India Fellow with a Master's Diploma from Ashoka Univeristy, India in July 2022 with a focus of Cognitive Psychology and Arts. For more details, check my CV or send me an email.

Research Projects

Understanding the phenotypic overlap between Parkinson's Disease (PD) and Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD)

  • Studying the phenotypic overlap between ASD and PD using Autism Quotient (AQ), Montreal Cognitive Assesment (MoCA) and Sleep Data.
  • First work that is studying this problem in an Indian cultural and environmental context and differentiating between diagnosed Parkinson's and Parkisonism.
  • Collected data for 330 people: 110 Parkison's disease patients and 110 each for the control sets : other motor disease patients and neurotypicals.
  • Working on analysis (using Jamovi) of collected data to publish our findings where we have intereting preliminary findings on striking differences between males and females.

    The Last Leaf
     The project captures a series of self portraits containing symbolism where I document my journey through denying, fighting and accepting my illness. It follows a coloring scheme from dark (Baroque) and grotesque to light and beautiful (Romanticism). There is a transition from black to dull white and grey to colorful and finally white which consists of all colors. My journey from denial to acceptance has been portrayed through the color transition. I've also written 3 poems reflecting over the three phases of the journey. For the photo portraits, I've used various paintings and photographs for inspiration and reference :

  • Death on Toilet by Francis Bacon
  • The Vitruvian Man by Leonardo da Vinci
  • The Scream by Edvard Munch
  • The Plan by Jenny Saville
  • The Morgue by Andres Serrano
    • The central vision of my project is to create awareness about fibroids and acknowledge the struggle that comes with it
